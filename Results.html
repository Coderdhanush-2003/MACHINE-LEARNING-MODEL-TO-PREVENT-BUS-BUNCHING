Results

Buses bunch up when delays snowball, leading to overcrowded clumps instead of evenly spaced service.

Bus bunching, where several buses arrive at a stop close together or even one after the other, can be a frustrating and inefficient experience for both riders and drivers. Luckily, there are several points to consider that can help prevent or minimize this happening:

Technology and Infrastructure:

  • Real-time Fleet Management: Implementing intelligent systems that track bus locations and traffic conditions allows for dynamic adjustments to schedules and dispatching. This can help buses adjust their speed to maintain even headways and prevent bunching.

  • Signal Priority: Giving buses priority at traffic lights through green wave systems or dedicated turn lanes can significantly improve travel times and reduce bunching caused by traffic delays.

  • Exclusive Bus Lanes: Dedicated lanes for buses allow them to flow freely, independent of general traffic congestion, which can significantly reduce bunching and improve overall service reliability.

  • Boarding Efficiency: Measures like pre-payment fares, multiple doors for faster boarding/alighting, and improved passenger information systems can speed up stops and prevent delays that contribute to bunching.

Operational Strategies:

  • Scheduled Holding Points: Designating specific points along the route where buses can wait if running ahead of schedule helps maintain consistent headways and prevent bunching at stops.

  • Dynamic Headway Control: Utilizing algorithms or communication systems to adjust the spacing between buses in real-time based on traffic conditions and passenger load can help prevent bunching and smooth out service.

  • Driver Training and Incentives: Educating drivers about how to manage their speed and maintain headways, and potentially offering incentives for consistent performance, can contribute to preventing bunching.

Passenger Awareness:

  • Real-time Information: Providing accurate arrival and headway information through apps, signs, and announcements can help passengers adjust their waiting times and avoid overcrowded buses, reducing the impact of bunching.

  • Spreading Ridership: Encouraging off-peak travel through fare discounts or other incentives can help spread passenger load throughout the day and reduce the likelihood of bunching during peak hours.
